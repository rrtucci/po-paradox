Abstract
Donald Rubin’s Potential Outcomes theory makes two key assumptions that we shall
call SUTVA and CIA. In this brief letter, we question whether those two assumptions
can hold simultaneously.

1 Introduction
Donald Rubin’s Potential Outcomes (PO) theory (a.k.a. Rubin’s Causal Model) (Ref.
[3]) is a popular method for doing causal inference (CI). PO theory is explained in
numerous textbooks (Refs.[2, 1, 4]).
PO theory makes two key assumptions that we shall call SUTVA and CIA. In
this brief letter, we question whether those two assumptions can hold simultaneously.
